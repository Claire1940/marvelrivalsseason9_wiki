<Section icon={Shield} title="The Season 9 Team-Up System Overhaul">

Marvel Rivals Season 9: The Mystery of Thebes introduces a massive architectural shift to the game's signature Team-Up mechanic. Previously, Team-Up abilities rotated on a seasonal schedule and required both characters to be locked in by teammates. In Season 9, that restrictive system is gone. 

**Video Highlights:**

<YouTubeEmbed videoId="YusekrZKvwQ" title="Marvel Rivals Season 9 Vanguard Team-Ups" />

Under the new system, every Vanguard possesses two distinct Team-Up loadouts. You equip one at a time and can swap them freely inside any spawn room. Crucially, the **Base Effect** of these abilities is always active—even if your partner hero is not in the match. If a teammate does select your partner, the ability automatically upgrades to its **Enhanced Effect**.

To offset the removal of flat Team-Up Anchor stats (which used to grant passive health or damage boosts), NetEase has adjusted individual character health pools and introduced Regenerative Shields.

| Old Team-Up System | New Season 9 Overhaul |
|:--- |:--- |
| Required partner active on team | Works independently with **Base Effects** |
| Granted flat Anchor stat bonuses | Anchor bonuses removed; balanced individually |
| Abilities rotated seasonally | Permanent dual loadouts balanced via patches |
| Fixed singular synergy | Two selectable loadouts per Vanguard |

<Callout type="warning" title="Anchor Stat Removal Warning">
Be aware that many Vanguards have lost 50 to 150 flat bonus health that was previously baked into their old Team-Up Anchor roles. You must rely on the new Regenerative Shields and active healing windows to survive frontline pressure.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="The Regenerative Shield Meta Shift">

To survive the removal of flat Team-Up Anchor health bonuses, multiple frontline Vanguards have had their health pools restructured with the new **Regenerative Shield** mechanic. This shield functions exactly like base health for healing and damage scaling, but it regenerates automatically after you avoid damage for 5 seconds.

```
[Take Damage] ──> [Avoid Damage for 5 Seconds] ──> [Auto-Regen Starts] ──> [Fully Restored in 5 Seconds]
```

This structural shift requires Vanguards to play with a disciplined "dive, block, retreat, and recharge" rhythm rather than sitting in chokepoints indefinitely.

<Steps>
 <Step num="1" title="Initiate and Absorb">
 Engage the enemy team using your base health pool and active cooldowns to absorb initial burst damage.
 </Step>
 <Step num="2" title="Deploy Team-Up Shields">
 Trigger your equipped Vanguard Team-Up ability (such as Doctor Strange's Psionic Vortex or Rogue's Defensive Stance) to mitigate secondary pressure.
 </Step>
 <Step num="3" title="Tactical Retreat">
 When your base health drops, use natural map cover or destructible terrain to disengage from the line of sight for exactly 5 seconds.
 </Step>
 <Step num="4" title="Recharge and Re-engage">
 Allow your Regenerative Shield to fully recover over the next 5 seconds, then cycle back into the fight with a refreshed health buffer.
 </Step>
</Steps>
